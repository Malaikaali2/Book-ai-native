Assessment Rubrics: Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics Course

Overview​

This document provides detailed assessment rubrics for all assignments, projects, and learning outcomes in the Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics course. The rubrics are designed to provide clear, consistent, and fair evaluation criteria while supporting student learning and growth.

Assessment Philosophy​

Our assessment approach emphasizes:

  • Learning-Centered: Assessments support learning rather than merely measure it
  • Authentic: Evaluations mirror real-world robotics challenges and professional practice
  • Formative: Regular feedback helps students improve throughout the course
  • Competency-Based: Focus on demonstrating specific skills and knowledge
  • Equitable: Clear criteria and multiple ways to demonstrate understanding

Assessment Categories​

1. Technical Implementation (40% of course grade)​

Evaluates students' ability to implement, debug, and optimize robotic systems.

2. Problem-Solving and Analysis (30% of course grade)​

Assesses analytical thinking, system design, and problem-solving approaches.

3. Communication and Documentation (20% of course grade)​

Evaluates technical communication, documentation, and presentation skills.

4. Professional Practice (10% of course grade)​

Assesses collaboration, ethics, and professional conduct.

Final Grade Calculation​

Grade Distribution​

  • Labs (40%): 13 weekly labs with varying weights
  • Assignments (40%): 13 major assignments with varying weights
  • Professional Practice (10%): Ongoing assessment throughout course
  • Participation (10%): Class participation, discussions, and peer reviews

Grade Scale​

  • A (93-100%): Exceptional performance with advanced understanding and implementation
  • A- (90-92%): Excellent performance with strong understanding and implementation
  • B+ (87-89%): Very good performance with solid understanding and implementation
  • B (83-86%): Good performance with adequate understanding and implementation
  • B- (80-82%): Satisfactory performance with basic understanding and implementation
  • C+ (77-79%): Adequate performance with limited understanding
  • C (73-76%): Marginal performance with minimal understanding
  • C- (70-72%): Below minimum standards with poor understanding
  • D (60-69%): Inadequate performance with limited understanding
  • F (Below 60%): Unacceptable performance with minimal understanding

Feedback and Improvement​

Feedback Timeline​

  • Labs: Feedback within 1 week of submission
  • Assignments: Feedback within 2 weeks of submission
  • Projects: Feedback within 3 weeks of submission
  • Professional Practice: Ongoing feedback throughout the course

Revision Policy​

  • Students may revise and resubmit lab assignments for up to 20% grade improvement
  • Major assignments may be revised with instructor approval and within 2 weeks of feedback
  • Projects may be revised for minor improvements with instructor approval

Appeals Process​

  • Students may appeal grades by providing evidence of grading errors
  • Appeals must be submitted within 1 week of grade posting
  • Appeals are reviewed by instructor and department chair

Special Accommodations​

Accessibility​

  • All assessments are designed to be accessible to students with disabilities
  • Alternative formats and extended time accommodations available as needed
  • Universal design principles applied to all assessments

Diverse Learning Needs​

  • Multiple ways to demonstrate understanding
  • Flexible assessment formats where possible
  • Individual consultation available for students with diverse needs

Quality Assurance​

Assessment Validity​

  • All assessments align with learning objectives
  • Rubrics are reviewed and validated by subject matter experts
  • Regular assessment review and updates based on student performance

Consistency​

  • Inter-rater reliability checks for subjective assessments
  • Regular calibration meetings for grading consistency
  • Detailed rubrics to ensure consistent application
